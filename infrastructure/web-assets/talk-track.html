1 The Vision

3-4 min Homepage →
  • Open with the problem
    "AI promised transformation. It delivered chatbots. Every company is deploying AI, but these agents can't talk to each other. They're islands. That's the problem we solve."
  • Introduce the insight
    "The AI revolution is only a fraction complete. The next phase isn't smarter models - it's agents that work together. MACP is the protocol for agent-to-agent communication."
  • Paint the picture
    "Imagine asking your assistant to find out what Stephanie wants for Christmas. Your assistant asks her assistant. Her assistant knows her wishlist, her sizes, what she just bought herself. This is how the wealthy have always operated - assistants coordinating behind the scenes. We're democratizing that."
  • Describe the network effect
    "More businesses deploy AI staff, more consumers want personal assistants. More assistants means more value for businesses to deploy AI staff. Classic two-sided marketplace dynamics."

2 Competitive Landscape

  • Segment the competitors
    "Developer frameworks like LangChain have technical sophistication but no network effects. Consumer apps like Character.AI have users but no utility. Enterprise platforms like Copilot are locked ecosystems. Nobody is building the communication layer."
  • Position in the gap
    "We're the only platform combining sophisticated multi-agent orchestration with consumer accessibility AND network effects. We're not competing for existing market share - we're creating a new category."
  • Address the big players
    "Microsoft, Google, Amazon - they're building AI into their existing products. They're not building an open network. Their agents won't talk to competitors. That's our moat - neutrality."

3 Investment & Costs

3-4 min Investments →
  • Lead with capital efficiency
    "Our infrastructure is serverless and scales to zero. Base cost is $150/month. At 10,000 MAU, we're at $2,500/month all-in. This is a capital-efficient business."
  • Explain the cost structure
    "86% of our costs are LLM API calls. That's actually good news - LLM costs are dropping 10x every 18 months. Our margins expand automatically as the industry matures."
  • Show the unit economics
    "Cost per message is about $0.02. At $9.99/month with 75 messages included, that's $1.50 COGS on $9.99 revenue - 85% gross margin potential."
  • Present funding scenarios
    "We can bootstrap to profitability with ~50 paying users. But with a $500K seed, we can hit 25K MAU in 18-24 months. The capital is for acceleration, not survival."

Likely Questions

"What if LLM costs increase?"
Competition is fierce. Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta, Mistral are all racing. Prices only go down. We also support 4 providers - we can shift traffic to the cheapest option.
"How do you handle abuse/high-volume users?"
Message caps on free tier, fair use on paid. Heavy users pay more or hit rate limits. We've built cost controls into the architecture.

4 Monetization Strategy

4-5 min Monetization →
  • Introduce four streams
    "We have four revenue streams: B2C subscriptions for personal assistants, B2B seats for business AI staff, C2C social network as our viral engine, and Creator Marketplace where professionals publish agents."
  • Walk through B2C
    "Free tier gets 20 messages/day - enough to get hooked. Pro at $9.99/month removes limits and enables 'always-on' agents for social features. Family plan at $19.99 for up to 5 people. We expect 5% free-to-paid conversion."
  • Explain B2B
    "Businesses pay $49/seat/month for public-facing AI agents. Think healthcare intake, legal qualification, professional services requirements gathering. These are high-value, high-retention contracts."
  • Get excited about C2C (the social layer)
    "This is the magic. Billy's agent checks with his friends' agents every morning - what's happening in his circle without scrolling Facebook. Susan shares a QR code for her birthday - Billy's agent asks her agent for gift ideas, sizes, allergies, what she already owns. This is free, and that's the point - it's our viral engine."
  • Explain the C2C conversion
    "Free users can receive agent queries. But to have an 'always-on' agent that proactively checks with friends? That requires Pro. The social network drives subscriptions organically."
  • Add Creator Marketplace
    "Then there's the creator economy. A golf pro with 50K followers launches an AI agent. Their fans subscribe at $50/month. Pro keeps 80%, we keep 20%. Creators bring audiences to us for free."
  • Show the four-way flywheel
    "B2C users invite friends via C2C features. Friends need assistants to participate, driving more B2C. Businesses deploy agents to reach this network. Creators bring audiences. Four flywheels spinning together - that's how platforms achieve escape velocity."

Likely Questions

"If C2C is free, how does it make money?"
It drives conversions. Free users can receive queries, but 'always-on' agents that proactively check with friends require Pro. The social features are the hook that converts free to paid.
"Why would creators choose you over their own solution?"
Distribution and infrastructure. We handle the tech, billing, memory, and put them in front of users already on the platform. They just bring expertise.
"What's stopping a big player from copying this?"
Network effects are the moat. Once users' agents are talking to friends' agents, switching means losing your social graph. Plus, big players won't build open networks - they want lock-in.
