The Viral Engine

C2C is free - and that's the point. Every user who shares their QR code brings friends into the network. Those friends need their own assistants to participate. Free users can receive queries, but Pro subscribers get "always-on" agents that can initiate contact and stay updated. The social network drives subscriptions organically.

Model Assumptions

📊 Conversion Rates

Conservative: 3% free→paid. Optimistic: 7% free→paid. Industry benchmarks: Spotify 3%, Dropbox 4%.

📉 Churn Rates

B2C: 5% monthly (conservative), 3% (optimistic). B2B: 2% monthly. Marketplace creators: 10% annually.

💰 LTV:CAC

Target 3:1 ratio. B2C CAC: $30. B2B CAC: $500. Marketplace CAC: $0 (creators bring audience).

📈 Viral Coefficient

Conservative: 0.3 (each user brings 0.3 users). Optimistic: 0.8 (agent sharing drives virality).

💵 COGS

LLM costs: 20-25% of revenue. Infrastructure: 5% of revenue. Payment processing: 3%. Total COGS: 28-33%.

🌍 Market Size

TAM: $50B (AI assistants). SAM: $5B (personal + business). SOM: $500M (early adopters, US/EU).

The Compounding Advantage

Four flywheels spinning together: B2C users invite friends via C2C social features (gift lists, scheduling). Friends need their own assistants to participate, driving more B2C subscriptions. B2B businesses deploy agents to reach this growing consumer network. Creators monetize audiences, bringing even more users. Each stream reinforces the others - this is how platforms achieve escape velocity.
